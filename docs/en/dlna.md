# Receive DLNA casts

DLNA is a common casting protocol. When a player on your phone finds a compatible playback device, it can send video, music, or photos to that device.

Many phone players include a **Cast** button, including popular Chinese video apps.

## Use TinyPlay as a receiver

Windows and macOS do not normally receive DLNA casts by themselves. TinyPlay includes a DLNA receiver, so the computer connected to your TV can appear in the cast-device list after TinyPlay is installed and running.

1. Make sure your phone and the computer running TinyPlay are on the same local network.
2. Tap **Cast** in the phone player and choose the computer running TinyPlay.
3. Choose a video. The computer plays it through the TV.

## Playback speed

The casting app may not provide a playback-speed control after casting. To change the speed, return to TinyPlay's **Remote** page on your phone and choose a speed there.

DLNA only sends the content to another device. It does not carry danmaku or solve playback restrictions imposed by protected content.
