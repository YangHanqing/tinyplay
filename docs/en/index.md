# Turn an idle PC into a living-room player 🏠

Connect an idle Windows PC, NUC, or Mac mini to your TV and run TinyPlay. Your phone becomes the remote: browse, search, scrub, and switch subtitles from a modern phone interface. Existing content and signed-in web services on the computer can be used on the big screen according to each service's rules.

TinyPlay is not another TV box to learn and does not require you to build a complete media library first. Connect Jellyfin, browse a NAS folder directly, or add an IPTV playlist — start with the content you already have.

## What TinyPlay can do 📺

TinyPlay is designed for a few common living-room setups:

- 🎬 Watch movies and other video
- 📡 Watch IPTV
- 📲 Receive casts from phones and other devices
- 🌐 Open supported websites on the computer and control them from your phone (currently a Chinese beta)

## Choose your starting point 🧭

### I already use a media server or NAS

If Emby, Jellyfin, or Plex is already running on your NAS or computer, start with [Connect a media server](/en/media-server).

### I only have folders of video files

You can play movies and TV episodes directly from a folder on your computer or NAS without building a poster wall first. TinyPlay lets you browse the folder, choose a video, and control playback from your phone.

If you want posters, categories, episode metadata, and resume history, set up a media library such as Jellyfin. The media server organises the files; TinyPlay gives you the phone browsing interface and plays them on the computer connected to the TV. See [Build a media library](/en/library).
